## Introduction to Element Appearance Styles

### [fill](../../reference/UI/fill.md)

Fill, similar to `background-color` in HTML5 or `color` for text.

Supports [solid color](../../reference/UI/paint/solid.md), [linear gradient](../../reference/UI/paint/linear.md), [radial gradient](../../reference/UI/paint/radial.md), [angular gradient](../../reference/UI/paint/angular.md), and [image fill](../../reference/UI/paint/image.md). Multiple fills can be stacked.

### [stroke](../../reference/UI/stroke.md)

Stroke, similar to `border-color` in HTML5.

Supports [solid color](../../reference/UI/paint/solid.md), [linear gradient](../../reference/UI/paint/linear.md), [radial gradient](../../reference/UI/paint/radial.md), [angular gradient](../../reference/UI/paint/angular.md), and [image pattern](../../reference/UI/paint/image.md). Multiple strokes and [sub-stroke styles](../../reference/UI/stroke.md#子描边属性) are supported.

Draw elements around the `around` point, similar to the anchor system in game engines.
